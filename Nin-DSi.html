Nintendo DSi / DSi XL

2008-2009

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-DSi-Bl-Open. 2011.
.commons.wikimedia.org (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Nintendo-DSi-Bl-Open.jpg)

The Nintendo DSi is the second generation of the Nintendo DS line of consoles. It was released in Japan on November 1, 2008 and worldwide in April 2009. The larger model named the Nintendo DSi XL released in Japan on November 21, 2009 and worldwide in March 2010. The original launch price of the DSi was $169.99 and $189.99 for the DSi XL. The core design of the DSi is similar to that of the DS Lite, however, the DSi features two digital cameras, supports internal and external storage, and connects to an online store that Nintendo coined the Nintendo DSi Shop. The new functionality was meant to lead the user to personalize their DSi. This was to encourage family members to buy indiviual DSi for each member of the family who wanted one instead of having one DSi per household. The DSi offers exclusive physical media along with DS games that include specific DSi features along with the ability to play original DS games. The only problem is that you cannot play earlier DS games on the DSi that require a GBA slot. The DSi and DSi XL have combined sold a total of 41 million units. Reviews for the DSi were overall positive, however there was some complaints due to the DSi's cameras low resolution and the inability to play games that require the GBA cartridge. The DSi was seen as the pinacle console in the DS family of consoles. It was followed up and succeeded by the new line of DS consoles called the Nintendo 3DS.
