Nintendo DS Lite

2006-2007

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-DS-Lite-Black-Open. 2011.
.commons.wikimedia.org (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Nintendo-DS-Lite-Black-Open.jpg)

The Nintendo DS Lite is the second generation of the DS line of consoles. It has a slimmer, brighter, and lighterweight design compared to the original Nintendo DS. It was released in Australia, North America, Europe, New Zealand, Singapore, and certain regions in South America, the Middle East, and East Asia. It has shipped a total of 93.86 million units worldwide. The original price of the DS Lite was $130. The DS Lite was considered the first console sold in South Korea by the Nintendo subsidiary, Nintendo of Korea, on January 18, 2007. Jang Dong-gun and Ahn Sung-ki (two popular Korean actors) were hired to help promote the console at its release. The DS Lite measured in at 2.91 in. tall, 5.2 in. wide, and 0.85 in. deep. The top screen is 256x192-pixel resolution and is capable of displaying a total of 262,144 colors. The top and touch screen have the same capability and hardware. However, the touch screen has a transparent analog touch sensor. The DS Lite has A/B/X/Y buttons on the face of the device along with a directional control pad, L/R shoulder buttons, a start and select button, a volume slider, and a power switch. The DS Lite comes with a sylus about 1 cm longer, and 2 mm thicker than the original DS sylus. It boasts stereo speakers and has ports for stereo headphones and an embedded mircrophone for voice recognition. The DS Lite is cross compatible with Game Boy Advance Games as well as original Nintendo DS game. To achieve this, the DS Lite has a DS slot on top of the console and a Game Boy slot on the bottom.
