Nintendo 3DS / 3DS XL

2011

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-3DS-AquaOpen. 2010.
.en.wikipedia.org (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_3DS)

The Nintendo 3DS is the successor to the Nintendo DS line of consoles. The 3DS was announced in March 2010 and first shown at E3 2010. The 3DS was first releaed in Japan on Febuary 26, 2011 and released worldwide on July 28, 2011. The 3DS released at $249 to a disappointing launch. The significant price led the 3DS to have drastically lower launch sales than expected. Due to this, Nintendo lowered the price to $169. An XL version of the 3DS was also released for $199.99 similarly to the DSi and DSi XL. The primary feature new to the 3DS was the stereoscopic 3D effects on the screen of the console without the use of 3D goggles. The 3DS also featured the new StreetPass and SpotPass tag modes made using the Nintendo Network. Augmented reality was implimented using the 3DS' 3D cameras. A virtual console was also added to the 3DS which allows users to play games originally released on older Nintendo gaming consoles. The Nintendo eShop made its debute on the 3DS. An application called the Miiverese was realeased on the 3DS and acted as a social networking service. The 3DS also had the ability to use applications like an internet browser, Netflix, Hulu Plus, and Youtube. Nintendo Video, a messaging app called Swapnote and Mii Maker are other notable applications available on the 3DS. The hardware of the 3DS was a noticable improvement to the DSi boasting 2 GB of eMMC flash memory compared to the DSi's 256 MB of internal flash memory, a dual-core ARM11 MPCORE based CPU clocked at 268 MHz. The GPU of the 3DS is a PICA200 clocked at 268 MHz. Lastly the 3DS featured the Circle Pad, a round nub-like analog stick and six-axis motion sensor that includes a 3-axis accelerometer and a 3-axis gyroscope.
