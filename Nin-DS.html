Nintendo DS

2004-2005

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-DS-Fat-Blue. 2004.
.en.wikipedia.org (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_DS)

The Nintendo DS was released globally across 2004 and 2005 and was meant to complement the Game Boy Advanced family and GameCube. However, due to the system's features and strong titles, sales, etc, it eventually established itself as the successor to the Game Boy series. The DS was the first Nintendo system to feature the ability to directly interact with other DS consoles over Wi-Fi within a specific range without the need to connect to an already established wireless network. The DS also released with Nintendo's new Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection. The DS was marketed as Nintendo's competating console to Sony's PlayStation Portable. The DS launched in North America on November 21, 2004 for US$149.99 and in various other markets around the world. The DS sold for 154.02 million units across all of its models. The DS's sucess introduced touchscreen controls and wireless online gaming to a wider range of audiences. It introduced a casual and portable gaming market which attracted non-gamers and gamers alike. The DS readied the world for the release of the Iphone in 2007. The desing of the DS was inspired by the Game & Watch line of games such as Donkey Kong and The Legends of Zelda. The lower screen accepted input from a sylus, touch by the user's fingers, and other accessories. The DS lanuched with a number of games, the most recognizable in NA including Super Mario 64 DS, Spider-Man 2, Metroid Prime Hunters: First Hunt, and Madden NFL 2005.
